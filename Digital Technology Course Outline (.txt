Digital Technology Course Outline (Years 7 & 8)

Assessment and Marking Schedule
This course primarily focuses on Formative Assessment (feedback and improvement) throughout the term, culminating in a Summative Assessment based on the final project (Weeks 8 & 9).
2. INIT: Final Project Goal (The "Digital Portfolio")
The Goal: Use your HTML/CSS skills in CodePen to build a 3-page website that presents all the work you've done in this 9-week course. (Note: In CodePen, you can simulate pages with links that jump to different sections of your one-page app, or create separate Pens and link them).
3. TASK: The Technical Checklist
To build a successful portfolio, you must include the following:
Homepage Section: A main section that introduces you and has links to your other sections.
CSS Styling: The project must be styled with a consistent colour scheme, fonts, and layout.
"My Code" Section:
Must include your final Python code (you can copy-paste it into the HTML).
Must have a write-up explaining what it does.
"My Research" Section:
Y7: Must embed your Data (the organised table from Google Sheets) and your AI Report (on everyday AI).
Y8: Must embed your Data Visualisation (the chart from Google Sheets) and your AI Report (on ethics).
Must include a short reflection on Digital Citizenship (e.g., "What I learned about fake news").
Published: The final site must be viewable via its public CodePen link.

Summative Assessment Rubric (Final Project - Weeks 8 & 9) using SOLO Taxonomy
The final project (Game/App Prototype or extended Week 7 To-Do List) will be assessed using the following SOLO criteria:
Criteria
Unistructural (≈ Level 3)
Multistructural (≈ Level 3 - Early 4)
Relational (≈ Competent Level 4)
Extended Abstract (≈ Mastery Level 4+)
Digital Content (HTML/CSS) (40%)
The CodePen exists but has broken links, missing content, and little to no CSS styling.
The CodePen meets all requirements. It has all required sections, links work, and all project work is embedded. CSS is used and functional.
The CodePen is polished, responsive, and easy to navigate. The design and layout show creativity and planning.
The design justifies choices based on User Experience (UX) principles (e.g., accessibility, contrast). The student may use advanced features (e.g., CSS animations) to enhance communication, not just for decoration.
Programming & CT (Python) (20%)
The Python project is included but is incomplete or has significant errors.
The Python project is complete, runs correctly, and meets the Y7/Y8 requirements (e.g., Y8 has loops).
The Python code is clean, well-commented, and includes extension features beyond the basic requirements.
The code generalises the solution. It uses efficient data structures (e.g., lists/dictionaries) instead of repetition and handles unexpected user input gracefully (robustness).
Data & AI Research (30%)
The Data and AI projects are missing or are very brief and show little understanding.
The Data and AI reports are complete and meet the Y7/Y8 requirements (e.g., Y8 has a chart and discusses ethics).
The reports show deep insight. The data is well-cleaned and the AI ethics discussion is well-reasoned.
The student critically evaluates the limitations of the dataset or the AI tool itself. They propose specific, actionable solutions to the ethical risks or data bias identified.
Digital Citizenship (Reflection) (10%)
The reflection is missing or is a single sentence.
The student includes a reflection that explains what they learned about digital citizenship.
The reflection connects concepts, explaining how digital citizenship relates to AI bias or data privacy.
The reflection advocates for a change in behaviour. It generalises the specific lesson to broader principles (e.g., "Digital Rights") that apply to future technologies, not just the ones studied.
